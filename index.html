Projects & Focus Areas

Research

We combine analytical theory, numerical simulation, and close collaboration with experimental groups to understand quantum matter.

🔬

Topological Quantum Matter

We study topological insulators, semimetals, and superconductors — classifying their symmetry-protected phases and predicting experimentally accessible signatures.

Weyl Semimetals Berry Phase Edge States
⚛️

Strongly Correlated Electron Systems

Mott insulators, high-Tc superconductors, and heavy-fermion compounds exhibit physics beyond single-particle descriptions. We develop field-theoretic and numerical tools to tackle this challenge.

Hubbard Model DMFT Spin Liquids
🌐

Moiré & Van der Waals Heterostructures

Flat bands in twisted bilayer graphene and TMDs host unconventional superconductivity and correlated insulating states. We map the phase diagram and examine proximity effects.

tBLG Flat Bands Moiré Physics
💡

Quantum Phase Transitions

We study quantum criticality, deconfined quantum critical points, and emergent gauge theories near zero-temperature phase transitions in frustrated magnets and itinerant systems.

Quantum Criticality RG Methods CFT
🧲

Unconventional Superconductivity

From cuprates to kagome metals, we investigate the pairing symmetry, pseudogap physics, and interplay between charge order and superconductivity using spectroscopic and transport probes.

d-Wave Pairing Kagome Metals CDW
🖥️

Computational Quantum Materials

We develop and apply quantum Monte Carlo, tensor network (DMRG/PEPS), and machine-learning methods to simulate correlated quantum materials beyond analytical reach.

QMC DMRG Neural Networks
